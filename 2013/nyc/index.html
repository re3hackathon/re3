About

We’re into justice and we believe in the power of a good story.

It’s time to expand the boundaries of justice-focused persuasive storytelling. We asked changemakers from nonprofits, foundations, and activist organizations to propose specific stories relating to complex issues like economic fairness, human rights and poverty. In parallel, we offered creative storytellers the chance to choose one of eight selected stories and work with top-notch teammates to communicate and create a paradigm shift around the issues that matter.

Over the Re3 StoryHack weekend, we innovated new ways of thinking and communicating these stories in language; written, visualized, performed, coded and more.

Results

Eight stories, nominated by changemakers, were selected for the Re3StoryHack weekend. Each changemaker completed a Story Brief which was sent to our teams of creatives. These offered background on the issue, the attitude shift that they hope to accomplish, and more context data to make the story grounded in fact. We also supported a group of emergent stories, with participants pitching ideas and building a team of hackers to join them.

Here is what Re3StoryHack creators built in just one weekend.

  • Half is Not Enough

    People who make minimum wage earn only half what they need to survive in New York City. Support a living wage for all.

  • Wage Island

    Is the story of a NYC which rises from a an unlivable minimum wages to a livable minimum wage told through a data sculpture.

  • From: Homeless to Houseless

    Over the course of 36 hours, we came together to tell a story about homelessness. The numbers we couldn't ignore revealed an invisible population: the working homeless. A job in New York City--or even two jobs--does not guarantee you a place to live. And so our perspective shifted: from homeless to houseless.

  • $2,700 a Month

    It's the system that's broken, not the people.

  •  #RealCostOf

    44 Million Americans are without sick days, and its a cost we all bare.

    Most Likely to Shift Perspective

  • #GetWellFL

    A social media campaign mobilizing people to demand paid sick leaves for all Floridians.

  • Run For Us

    A more representative government starts closer to home than you may think.

    Most Likely to Reach a New Audience, Audience Award

  •  34,000 Sold - End the Quota

    How human rights are traded for profit in the United States

    Audience Choice

  • Text and Found

    Change lives in 5 minutes or less. We can't change the whole system today, but we can provide a key service for the family members and loved ones of detained immigrants.

  •  15 Days

    In the United States, over 80,000 people live up to 23 hours a day, indefinitely, in tiny prison cells with no light or human contact—many of them sent or kept there for minor, nonviolent infractions as simple as having too many postage stamps.

    Best Execution

  •  Bullet Points

    As series of video vignettes which reveal the stories behind the statistics of gun violence. The videos—posted through a dedicated instagram account—can be a tool for leaders in the communities affected as well as informative for the general public.

    Humanizing Data

  • Every Shooting Is A Mass Shooting

    One bullet shatters more lives than you think.

  • Nobody Goes It Alone

    Recognizing the people who have our backs.

  • GIFs for Good

    #EmotionsAreEmotions. Before we can begin to solve the problem of poverty, we need to stop dividing each other into the "rich" and the "poor," the "haves" and the "have nots." Lets stop considering #richpeopleproblems and #poorpeopleproblems, and recognize the commonality in all of our problems.

  • Back To The Shutdown

    What if the Government Shutdown of 2013 was permanent? We went Back To The Shutdown to explore its long-term effects.
