What should I bring?

Bring whatever gear you'll need to hack with. Laptops with software, thumb drives and other external storage. For filmmakers especially, bring an extra laptop in case you need to process media files. Bring extra drives fully loaded so you don't need to ever wait in case one team member is transferring files. We'll have wifi, but bring your mifi (if you have one) as a backup. We also encourage you to download any data/software/documents before arriving to the site. Bring headphones and splitters, because you'll be working in a shared space. If you have video adapters, please bring them in case we have to connect your device to our projector. Bring anything else you can think of: phones, chargers, surge protectors, extension cords, mouse pads, pens, paper, business cards...

What should I prepare?

In talking with your team, you'll decide what assets you need to prepare (i.e., video, audio, text, etc.) and what development environments you'll need to set up. You may want to share a dropbox folder, or set up a URL or FTP ahead of time, and possibly register a domain. But plan this with your team.

Do I have to work non-stop?

No. The work space will be closed at 2AM on Saturday night. Hacking will resume again at 10:00 AM. It's wise for teams to decide a work schedule at the Kickoff.

What are the criteria for stories?

In most cases, we’re aiming for stories that shift a perspective/attitude of a member of the general public. This is an example of a simple “story” that we could imagine being able to create in a weekend. It's perfect because it takes a topical issue - racial profiling - and instead of pointing the finger at others asking "issue X to change", it gently asks us to look ourselves in the mirror.

What might stories be?

It could be anything! What you create is highly variable, and could include:

  • An infographic bringing attention to stunning stats about your issue.
  • A series of posters linking your issue to the best movies of 2013.
  • A short video that makes a touching statement.
  • A team that decides to film the public’s reactions to being put in a solitary confinement cell that they build in Union Square as a punishment to jaywalking.
  • An animation showing the change in demographics in the US and the (lack of) change among US representatives since 1776.
  • A mock newscast from 2030.
  • Lots of other better ideas that you will have!
