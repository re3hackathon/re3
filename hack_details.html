StoryHack Details

Welcome Story Crafters !

Thanks for confirming your attendance for the Re3 StoryHack. We're psyched to have you. Now the real fun begins! Please carefully read over details about the weekend.

We’re trying to make this as easy and painless as possible for you, while also ensuring that the StoryHack doesn’t fall into the trap of lots of talk about how to do something, with less of the doing. We hope with our pre-setting things up and grouping into teams, you’ll be able to craft resonant stories.

What do we mean by stories?

In most cases, we’re aiming for stories that shift a perspective/attitude of a member of the general public. This is an example of a simple “story” that we could imagine being able to create in a weekend. It's perfect because it takes a topical issue - racial profiling - and instead of pointing the finger at others asking "issue X to change", it gently asks us to look ourselves in the mirror.

What might stories be?

It could be anything! What you create is highly variable, and could include:

  • An infographic bringing attention to stunning stats about your issue.
  • A series of posters linking your issue to the best movies of 2013.
  • A short video that makes a touching statement.
  • A team that decides to film the public’s reactions to being put in a solitary confinement cell that they build in Union Square as a punishment to jaywalking.
  • An animation showing the change in demographics in the US and the (lack of) change among US representatives since 1776.
  • A mock newscast from 2030.
  • Lots of other better ideas that you will have!

FAQ

  • What should I bring?
  • Do I have to work non-stop?

What should I bring?

Bring any gear you need to make your best work. You'll need your laptops with software, thumb drives and other external storage if you think you'll need it. If you think you'll need any extra software, plugins or material, make sure you get it before arriving to the site. Filmmakers, bring your cameras and sound gear. If you need an extra laptop to process media files more quickly, bring that too. Our time is limited, so efficiency will be super important. Bring headphones and splitters, because you'll be working in a shared space. If you have video adapters, please bring them in case we have to connect your device to our projector. We'll have wifi access at Parsons, but if you have a mifi device, bring it as a backup. Bring anything else you can think of: phones, chargers, extension cords, mouse pads, pens, paper, business cards...

Do I have to work non-stop?

No. We'll have meals and snacks for breaks. You decide when you need to get up and stretch your legs. Day one of the event will end at 8pm but the work space will be open until 2AM. Teams are free to create their own work schedules and to work as long as they want until we get kicked out of the building. Hacking will resume again at 10:00 AM on Sunday morning. Presentations will begin at 4pm.
